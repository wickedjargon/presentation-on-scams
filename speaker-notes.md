# Key Trends:

* Overall, the CAFC saw increases in victimization with the Top 10 Frauds, with Bank Investigator Fraud (16.5%) and Phishing (6.8%) being the two largest increases. Extortion was the only fraud where a decrease was seen (10%).
* Investment scams make up 49% of the overall dollar loss ($643,716,736 CAD) reported to CAFC.
* Although the CAFC saw a decrease in victimization with Extortion scams, there was a significant increase in the dollar amount lost by victims ($8.6M increase).
* The CAFC saw the largest increase in losses with job scams ($17M increase).
* Individuals aged 19 and under are more likely to be victimized by fraud.
* The total amount lost by seniors makes up 40.3% of the overall dollar loss ($643,716,736 CAD) reported to CAFC.

# Slide 5

## Home for rent
- scammer posing as landlord posts an apartment for rent, usually at an unrealistically low price.
- scammer says the place is not available for viewing because he is out of town, but that the perspective renter can view the surrounding area and view images of the place
- scammer says once first and last month is paid that he will ship the keys to the renter
- of course, keys are never shipped

## Romance scam
- scammer posing as a prospective date, contacts someon
- usually a scammer will look for older people whose social media shows them by themselves in most/all photos
- scammer can also pose as celebrity reaching out to fans
- after the scammer knows that the victim is emotionally invested, he requests money
- scammer pretends as if there is *just one thing* left to pay for before the scammer can meet the victim in person, but of course, this never happens

## Tech support scam
- victim types in a URL, but misspells it, goes to a scam website. the website attempts to deceive the user into thinking there system is infected with a virus. a tech support number is provided. when the victim calls, they are presented with 1 year, 2 year, 3 year, and life time anti-malware options.
